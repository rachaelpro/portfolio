Photography

All of the photos in this portfolio are pictures I have taken myself, and they each represent a story from my life. Photography in itself isn’t a dedicated hobby of mine; I took most of these photos spontaneously on my phone camera. However, every image in my thorough camera roll contains memories and snapshots of things that are important to me. If you scrolled through my camera roll, you would see a lot–and I mean a lot–of pictures of my cat, dog, family, and friends. There are also quite a few poorly framed pictures of flowers growing in sidewalk cracks and animals crossing roads, as well as an entire gallery’s worth of sunsets–from perfectly captured mountainscapes to oversaturated blurs of light. What each of these photos reminds me of, the memories contained and evoked, and the significance of the subjects, is different between myself and every witness, and this lens of understanding is the same perspective I apply to my creative writing.

The importance of writing, to me, is to invoke the most complex and uncertain of moments, emotions, and experiences in a way that allows others to consider otherwise inexplicable moments. These photos likewise capture a moment and enact a form of communication that language cannot. Through each image, and especially in comparison to one another, a lot of what is important to me hopefully shows through. Just as each piece of writing has a photo thumbnail standing in place of its text until clicked, this page about my photography describes my photos in a snapshot of writing.
